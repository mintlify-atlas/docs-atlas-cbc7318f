title: "Quickstart"
description: "Get from a natural language prompt to a live Next.js app in minutes."
---

This guide walks you through creating your first app with Nextly — from signing in to viewing a live preview of your generated Next.js application.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign up or sign in">
    Go to [nextly.live](https://nextly.live) and create an account. Nextly uses Clerk for authentication — you can sign up with email or an OAuth provider.

    Once signed in, you land on the project dashboard where all your generated apps are listed.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a new project">
    Click **New Project** on the dashboard. You'll be taken to the project workspace with a prompt input field at the bottom of the screen.

    Each project maintains its own conversation history, so your follow-up prompts will have context from previous generations.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write your prompt">
    Type a description of the app you want to build. Be specific about layout, colors, and interactions. Here's an example to get you started:

    ```text
    Create a modern SaaS landing page for a project management tool. Include a hero
    section with a gradient background, headline "Manage Projects Effortlessly",
    subheadline, and two CTA buttons (Start Free Trial, Watch Demo). Add a features
    section with 6 feature cards in a grid layout, each with an icon, title, and
    description. Include a pricing section with 3 tiers (Starter $9/mo, Pro $29/mo,
    Enterprise custom). Footer with 4 columns: Product, Company, Resources, social
    icons. Use a blue and white color scheme with smooth animations on scroll.
    ```

    <Tip>
      The more specific your prompt, the better the output. Name your colors explicitly, describe the layout structure, and list the components you need. See [Writing effective prompts](/guides/writing-prompts) for detailed guidance.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select an AI model">
    Before submitting, choose the AI model for your generation:

    | Model | Key | Best for |
    |---|---|---|
    | Grok 4 Fast | `grok` | Fast generation, free tier |
    | GPT-5 Codex | `codex` | Complex logic and code quality |
    | Gemini 2.5 Flash | `gemini` | Balanced speed and capability |

    Grok 4 is the default and recommended model for beta users.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit and wait">
    Click **Generate**. Nextly sends your prompt to the generation pipeline:

    1. An Inngest background job starts — this ensures reliable processing even if the request takes a while
    2. An E2B sandbox environment spins up
    3. The AI agent writes your Next.js app files iteratively (up to 15 rounds)
    4. The dev server starts on port 3000 inside the sandbox

    Generation typically takes **2–10 minutes** depending on complexity and model load.

    <Info>
      You can leave the page and come back — the generation runs in the background and your project will update when it's ready.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="View the live preview">
    Once generation completes, a live preview URL appears in the chat panel. The URL looks like:

    ```
    https://<sandbox-id>.e2b.app
    ```

    Click it to open your running Next.js app. You can interact with it, test responsiveness, and share the URL with teammates.

    <Warning>
      Preview URLs expire after **10–30 minutes** due to sandbox budget constraints. Download your code immediately if you want to keep it — see [Downloading your code](/guides/downloading-code).
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Iterate with follow-up prompts">
    Not quite right? Type a follow-up in the same project:

    ```text
    Change the hero background to a dark navy gradient. Add a sticky navbar
    that changes background color on scroll. Make the CTA buttons use orange
    instead of blue.
    ```

    The agent has context from the previous generation and will update the existing files. See [Iterative development](/guides/iterative-development) for tips on effective follow-ups.
  </Step>
</Steps>
